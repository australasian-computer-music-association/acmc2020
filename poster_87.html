SILK

Matt Hitchcock

youtube
Session: Ambisonic Listening Room - Thursday 2020-07-09 9:30am-10:00am AEST
Stream Link: See ACMC Youtube Channel

Details

Abstract

This work is situated as an electroacoustic composition for fixed media, combining musical instruments and voice with electroacoustic elements using a single pitch from an African Kalimba processed in Native Instruments' Reaktor software. This 8-channel work (7 surround speakers and one Low Frequency Effects, marries a Kalimba ostinato with vocal performances. The use of timbre-space and compositional space are located as primary saliences, with spatial animation of phase-invariant spectral steering used as core to compositional coherence and circumspatial (Smalley, 2007) envelopment.

My concept of spectral steering is to slice a complex sound into spectral sub-layers, where each layer is then able to be steered into a different position in the speaker array. This is done in such a way there are zero phase anomalies meaning the psychoacoustic aspects allow the listener to sit inside a sound rather than to one side of it. Additional sonic manipulation may of course occur to each spectral layer, however at its simplest the term refers to the creation of spectral sub-bands and their flexible location in the circumspace.

The work is introspective in nature, using spatial proximity and sense of spatial complexity as core to the changing energies and textures within the composition. The ostinato is created purposefully with no definition or sense of bar-length / time signature – creating a floating but persistent pulse, and where a sense of ‘1’ becomes immaterial / unimportant to the success of the work. Experimenting with concepts of auditory streaming, a single sound is broken up into spectral layers, and then caused to change size and location, all the while placing the listener within that single note. Contrasting this, vocal performances are heard to weave throughout, placing emphasis on the contrasts, confluences and synergies arising, as well as the proximity to the listener forming part of the overall energy experience.

Bio

Matt is a multi-instrumentalist, musician, composer, sound designer, recording, mixing and mastering engineer, album producer, and general “mucker-abouter” with all things musical and technical. With a long history of performing, recording and producing, he also now invents strange ways of making sound (e.g. spectral discombobulation and surround steering of spectral sub-elements), ways of listening to surround (e.g. processes for surround in stereo headphones), as well as generating compositions using anything that makes sound. It makes sound? It’s in. Since 2008, Matt has been heading up the Queensland Conservatorium Griffith University Music Technology program in his role as program director, curriculum developer, mentor and teacher after starting a career in academia in 2001 following a long career as a performing, composing and producing musician.
